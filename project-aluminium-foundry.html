Project P — 008

Aluminium
Foundry

A backyard foundry built from a repurposed hoover and 3D-printed patterns — proof you can manufacture almost anything with enough ingenuity and the right heat.

2020 Personal Project Metal Casting
01

Overview

One of the earliest projects — and the one that first locked in the idea that you don't need a workshop to manufacture real metal parts, you just need to understand the process.

The foundry itself was built from a repurposed hoover (its motor pressed into service as a forced-air supply) and 3D-printed components. Sand-casting moulds were produced from 3D-printed pattern blanks: design the part on a computer, print the pattern, press it into bonded sand to form the cavity, pour molten aluminium, knock out the casting.

It bridged digital and physical manufacturing in a way few hobby projects do: parametric CAD on one end, a glowing crucible on the other. The casts came out — proof of concept demonstrated.

Year 2020
Material Aluminium
Method Sand casting
Patterns 3D-printed
Air Supply Repurposed hoover
02

Process

P — 01
Furnace Build

A small furnace built from refractory materials and 3D-printed structural components. A repurposed hoover supplies forced air to the burner, raising flame temperature enough to bring aluminium past its melting point of around 660 °C.

P — 02
Pattern Making

Parts were first designed in CAD and 3D-printed as solid pattern blanks. Printing the patterns directly — rather than carving them — turns the design step into something you can iterate cheaply.

P — 03
Sand Moulding

Patterns were pressed into bonded casting sand to form the negative cavity. The mould is split, the pattern withdrawn, runners and risers are cut into the sand, and the mould is reassembled ready to receive metal.

P — 04
Pour & Knock-Out

Aluminium melts in a crucible inside the furnace, gets poured into the mould, and is left to cool. Once solidified the mould is broken away (sand-casting is destructive by design) and the rough casting comes out — sprue trimmed, surface cleaned.
