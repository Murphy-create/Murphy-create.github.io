Projects

P — 001
Final Year Project — Automated DNA Amplification Platform

Designed and built an automated platform for biological assay procedures (LAMP and PCR DNA amplification). A primary motor spins an offset disk, generating centrifugal forces to move liquids; a secondary motor reorients the force vector under closed-loop PID control. An integrated heater drives the amplification reaction, while a camera detects colour changes and reports results to the master node. The master node manages disk inventory, instruction sequencing, inter-node communication, and timing.

P — 006
Heart-Rate Sensor Proof of Concept (2022)

Built a proof-of-concept optical heart-rate sensor using an LDR, green LED, and Arduino. The device illuminates the skin and measures voltage variations across the LDR, applying a 5-point moving average to smooth the signal. Successfully detected pulse peaks corresponding to blood-flow changes; early iterations did not compensate for ambient light, introducing baseline drift. SpO₂ measurement attempts were inconclusive but provided a strong basis for further iteration.
