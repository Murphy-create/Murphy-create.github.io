Project P — 003

PCB
Business Card

A fully custom PCB the size of a business card — five tactile buttons, a Charlieplexed LED matrix, and an ATmega328P-AU that turns the whole thing into a playable game console.

2023 Personal Project Embedded Electronics
01

Overview

A business card that introduces itself — and then lets you play it. The brief was simple: produce something pocket-sized that proves an interest in electronics, embedded firmware, and product-level design all at once.

The constraint of "business card scale" forced every decision. The board runs on a single 3.3V coin cell. Five tactile buttons drive game input. A Charlieplexed LED matrix provides the display — chosen specifically because it can drive far more LEDs than there are GPIO pins. The whole thing runs on an ATmega328P-AU microcontroller in firmware written close to the metal.

A statement piece at the intersection of electronics and product design — and a working example of building inside tight power, space, and pin-count budgets.

MCU ATmega328P-AU
Power 3.3V coin cell
Input 5 tactile buttons
Display Charlieplexed LED matrix
Year 2023
02

Design Decisions

D — 01
Why Charlieplexing

Charlieplexing exploits the tri-state nature of microcontroller pins to drive N × (N−1) LEDs from just N pins. On a card-sized board with limited GPIO and zero room for an LED driver IC, it's the only way to get a meaningful display.

D — 02
Why a Coin Cell

A 3.3V coin cell keeps the board flat, thin, and self-contained — no USB, no charging, no external power. The trade-off: every component must tolerate the voltage and the firmware must respect the current budget.

D — 03
Why the ATmega328P

Well-documented, cheap, hand-solderable in its QFP package, and capable enough to run interactive games while sipping power. A pragmatic choice over more exotic MCUs when the goal is to actually finish the thing.

D — 04
Why It's a Game

Anyone can hand you a card. A card that plays a game makes itself memorable — and proves the embedded firmware, button debouncing, and display refresh actually work in practice, not just in theory.
