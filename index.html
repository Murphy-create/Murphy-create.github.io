Projects

P — 001 · Featured
DNA Amplification Automation System
Final Year Project · 2025

Automated LAMP and PCR biological procedures — techniques used to amplify DNA — within a fully closed-loop mechatronic system. A main motor spins an offset disk to generate centrifugal forces that drive liquid movement through microfluidic channels. A secondary motor, controlled via closed-loop PID, dynamically alters the direction of centrifugal force to route reagents through different stages. A heater manages thermal cycling for DNA amplification; a camera detects colorimetric results and reports back to a master control node. The master node maintains a registry of disk types and associated instruction sequences, orchestrates inter-node messaging, and manages precise timing across the entire process.

PID Control Embedded Systems Mechatronics Computer Vision Microfluidics Python
