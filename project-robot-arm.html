6-DOF
Robot Arm

A multi-phase robotics project — three iterations, each one a deeper test of inverse kinematics, closed-loop control, and precision drive trains.

2024 — Present Personal Project ● In development
01

Overview

A robot arm is one of the canonical mechatronic problems — coordinated multi-axis motion, kinematic transforms, mechanical compliance, and control all converge in a single system. This project tackles it in iterations, each prototype scoped to validate one layer of the problem before moving on.

The progression is deliberate: prove the math first, then the control, then the drive train. Each version inherits the lessons of the last.

Status Iteration 3 — in design
Axes 5 (current target)
Drive NEMA 17 steppers
Reduction Twin cycloidal + capstan
02

Iterations

V — 01
Desktop Proof of Concept

A small-scale prototype built to validate the inverse kinematics calculations end-to-end. Goal: prove that the math correctly maps target poses to joint angles before investing in better hardware. Confirmed the IK chain worked on real geometry.

V — 02
Stepper + Encoder Closed Loop

Second prototype using stepper motors and magnetic encoders. Introduced closed-loop control on each axis — moving past open-loop stepping into position-verified motion. A first taste of dealing with backlash, missed steps, and sensor drift in a real drive train.

V — 03
5-Axis Final Design

Current iteration: a 5-axis arm driven by NEMA 17 steppers. Twin cycloidal drives at the base joint and lower arm provide high reduction with minimal backlash. A capstan drive handles the upper arm — chosen for its zero-backlash, smooth power transmission characteristics.

V — 04
Why These Drive Types

Cycloidal drives offer compact, high-ratio reduction with excellent shock resistance — ideal for the heavily-loaded base and lower arm. Capstan drives use cable-and-pulley reduction with effectively zero backlash, well suited to the lighter upper arm where precision matters more than torque density.
